About me

Tl/dr:Tech writer with experience writing in a variety of genres, with special interests in software and web publishing. Comfortable working with code in a variety of languages and contexts. Experience collaborating with PMs, developers, and designers in an Agile environment. Proficient editor. Eminently humble.

I've been interested in reading and writing most of my life. I selected a college major I hoped would guide me to a career writing and editing. At some point in my college career, I realized that I preferred writing and editing in a style that was economical, precise, and useful.

This realization coincided with a burgeoning interest in programming and web development. Thanks to some classes in the Editing and Publishing program, I discovered technical writing. I enjoyed the process of gaining knowledge of technical subjects and communicating them in language as clear and concise as possible. And the rest is history.

In my free time, I'm soccer fan and (very amateurish) player. In my free time, I like lifting weights and running. I read, too.

I graduated in December 2023 with a BA in Editing and Publishing and minors in Linguistics Computing, Digital Humanities and Technology, and Philosophy. My overall GPA is 3.99. Here's some info about my degree and minors (because they aren't all self explanatory):

BA: Editing and Publishing

Editing and Publishing is a unique major which frames writing and editing as professional applications of linguistics, and it has helped me approach writing and editing with clarity, accuracy, and attention to detail. It also provided me with training on technical editing and a variety of genres of professional writing.

Minor: Linguistics Computing

Linguistics Computing is a minor focused on performing linguistic analysis with computational tools and techniques. Skills: Web scraping, corpus compilation and analysis, sentiment analysis, syntax analysis, etc. (all in Python).

Minor: Digital Humanities and Technology

Digital Humanities and Technology is a minor primarily comprising web development and design classes. Relevant skills I've learned in this minor are the Adobe Suite (especially InDesign); front-end web development with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript; and CRUD apps with MySQL and PHP.

Minor: Philosophy

Philosophy introduced me to a variety of philosophical thinkers and traditions, and it also introduced me to formal logic, which I found very helpful in establishing a base of programming understanding.
